Automatic Embroidery Texture Synthesis for Garment Design and Online Display

基于纹理合成的刺绣图案生成算法的设计与实现

刺绣是一项传统的手工艺术。借助于工业技术，刺绣广泛用于服装的设计与生产中，从而赋予服装以独特的美学意义。随着信息技术的发展，服装的数字化成为未来的发展趋势。刺绣作为服装中的重要元素，将不可避免地参与到服装的虚拟生产场景当中，作以数字化的呈现。然而现有的算法解决方案，包括深度神经网络以及交互式的基于图像的风格化绘制方法等，或者难以生成清晰整齐的线迹结果，或者依赖于复杂的用户的交互操作。本文意在通过一种解决方案，使用纹理合成技术，将输入图稿自动化地转化为对应的刺绣结果，帮助解决刺绣虚拟可视化方面的现存问题。
本文介绍了一种基于纹理合成技术的自动化框架，用于将任意的输入图稿，转化为刺绣风格的工艺作品，从而用于虚拟服装的设计与在线展示场景。在给定输入图稿与素材纹理图的情况下，本文的算法首先从输入图稿中，运用图像分割的方法提取刺绣区域。每个经过提取的刺绣区域都自动地分配有某种刺绣线迹类型以及绣线颜色。随后，本文通过基于刺绣线迹的纹理合成方法，使用素材纹理图填充这些区域。对于每个区域，本文的方法可以保留合成前后的色彩相似性，同时也保留每种刺绣线迹类型的贯有特征。与现行方法相比，本文的方法可以在刺绣生成结果当中自动化地再现刺绣的原有线迹细节。此外，该方法可以有效地接受不同类型的输入图稿，从而仅需要简单的交互操作，就可以将刺绣图案的生成效果快速预览到数字服装上，加快从设计到成型的工作流。本文通过充分的实验和对比来验证了算法的有效性。

THE DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION OF TEXTURE SYNTHESIS BASED EMBROIDERY GENERATION ALGORITHM

Embroidery is a traditional art form of handicraft. It is widely used in garment design and production due to the industrial embroidery technology, and provides a unique aesthetic dimension to garments. With the development of information technology, garment digitalization becomes a growing trend, and the visualization of an embroidery design is irreplaceable in the garment virtual production scenario. Existing works deal with this problem via methods such as deep neural networks or interactive image-based artistic rendering methods, which generates ambiguous or distorted stitches in the results or require complex user interaction. We argue that this problem can be solved by automated style-transfer from design images to their embroidery-style correspondences using texture synthesis, and thus design an automated tool for embroidery design visualization.
We introduce an automatic texture synthesis based framework to convert an arbitrary input image into embroidery style art for garment design and online display. Given an input image and some reference textures, we first extract embroidery regions from the input image using image segmentation. Each segmented region is labeled with a stitch style and its embroidery color automatically. Then, we fill these regions with embroidery reference textures via a stitch-style-based texture synthesis method. For each region, our approach can keep the similarity of color before and after synthesis and the consistence of the stitch style. Compared to existing approaches, our method is able to generate digital embroidery patterns with faithful details automatically. Moreover, it can accept diverse input images effectively, enabling a fast preview of the embroidery patterns synthesized on digital garments via simple interaction, and therefore accelerate the workflow from design to production. We validate our method through extensive experiments and comparisons.
